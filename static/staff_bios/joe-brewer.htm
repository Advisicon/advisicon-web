Joe Brewer, MCP, MCTS, MBA
Director of Advisicon University

Joe is the Director of Advisicon University. He is an energetic, ethical and results oriented Executive with a proven success of team building, leadership and return on investment results. He executes a systems-thinking approach to change, change-management, Project Management, Program Management, Portfolio Management, Operations, Marketing and strategic planning incorporating a team designed approach to success. His Project Management, teaching and Human Resources background enable him to function well in a classroom environment while capable of interfacing at all levels of management.

Joe holds a MBA from George Fox University, two BS degrees in Project Management and Organizational Design and Leadership from George Fox University, coupled with three Associate degrees in Human Resources, Criminal Justice and General sciences. Joe has extensive experience teaching, through his twenty-five years of US Air Force service and corporate positions. He is versed in Project Management methodologies, Business Process Mapping and Management, Organizational Design, Six Sigma and Lean initiatives. In addition to this, Joe joins the Advisicon team as the lead DiSC® Communication Styles instructor, assisting companies and teams with internal communication hurdles and increasing overall project and team efficacy and profitability.

He is a Microsoft Certified Professional in Client Software, as well as a Microsoft Certified Technology Specialist in Microsoft Project. He is near completing his PMP certification.

Joe brings extensive mentoring and leadership qualities to the Advisicon team while remaining passionate about business intelligence, process improvement, mentorship, servant leadership, and overall quality improvement; his overall objective being the maintenance of quality while optimizing efficiency.

Joe has extensive background in project management and quality program leadership to include international and government environments as well as non-profits. He presently sits on two Boards of Directors for non-profits: The Camp Rosenbaum Foundation and the Enlisted Association of the National Guard Oregon (where he previously served as President).
